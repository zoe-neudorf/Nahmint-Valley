About This Map
Overview

This interactive map shows proposed and historical logging activity in the Nahmint watershed. The Nahmint valley has some of the largest tracks of remaining unprotected old growth forest on Vancouver Island. In 1990 this was recognized and the area was designated a special management zone to protect local biodivserity. However, in 2019 an investigation was undertaken by the Ministry that found BCTS was failing to comply with old-growth protection standards and that too much of the valley's old growth had already been logged. Today, blocks continue to be put up for auction in the Nahmint. For more information on the Nahmint please visit the Ancient Forest Alliance website .

Included Layers

Red polygons indicate areas where logging is proposed to take place. Pink polygons have been created to highlight areas where proposed cutblocks are placed in TAP Priority Deferral areas. Old Growth Management Areas are shown using Black Horizontal Lines. These are areas where logging is prevented or restrained. Green polygons are areas that the Technical Advisory Panel (TAP) has identified as Priority Deferral Areas. Brown polygons show small (lighter) to large (darker) old growth stands. The Yellow previously logged layer shows polygons from the Consolidated Cutblocks dataset, and carefully selected VRI data under 100 years of age. Critical Habitat for federally listed species at risk is identified in Blue. For more information click on your polygon of interest and toggle layers on and off using the legend.
